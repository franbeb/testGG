Quebec Claim Rare Earth Elements Project

James Bay Region, Quebec, Canada

A strategic rare earth elements (REE) project in Quebec's mineralogy-rich James Bay region, targeting critical elements essential for the renewable energy and high-tech manufacturing sectors.

Project Overview

The Quebec Claim Rare Earth Elements Project encompasses 5,800 hectares in the James Bay region of northern Quebec, Canada. The project is strategically located in a region with excellent geological potential for rare earth element mineralization, especially the highly sought-after heavy rare earth elements (HREE).

Initial exploration has identified several carbonatite and alkaline intrusive complexes with significant rare earth element enrichment. Surface sampling has returned total rare earth oxide (TREO) values ranging from 1.5% to 4.2%, with a favorable distribution of critical rare earths including neodymium, praseodymium, dysprosium, and terbium.

Golden Goose Exploration acquired the Quebec Claim property in 2022 and has conducted preliminary geological mapping, geophysical surveys, and surface sampling programs. The company is advancing the project with a focus on defining potential high-grade zones and understanding the mineralogical characteristics of the REE mineralization.

Geology & Mineralogy

The Quebec Claim project is situated within the Superior Province of the Canadian Shield, characterized by Archean and Proterozoic rocks with multiple intrusive complexes. The primary targets are alkaline and carbonatite intrusions that have undergone extensive enrichment in rare earth elements.

Geological mapping has identified three main intrusive complexes within the property boundaries, each showing distinctive REE mineralization styles:

  • North Zone: A carbonatite complex with abundant bastnäsite and monazite mineralization
  • Central Zone: An alkaline intrusive with xenotime and allanite as the primary REE-bearing minerals
  • South Zone: A peralkaline granite with elevated concentrations of heavy rare earth elements

Mineralogical studies have shown that the REE mineralization is predominantly in the form of bastnäsite, monazite, and xenotime, with favorable liberation characteristics that suggest amenability to conventional processing methods.

Exploration & Development

Golden Goose Exploration has implemented a systematic exploration program at Quebec Claim, including:

  • Detailed geological mapping and surface sampling across all three target zones
  • Airborne magnetic and radiometric surveys covering the entire property
  • Ground gravity and induced polarization (IP) surveys over priority targets
  • Trenching and channel sampling of outcropping mineralization
  • Preliminary mineralogical and metallurgical characterization studies
  • Environmental baseline studies and stakeholder engagement

Initial exploration results have been encouraging, with highlight findings including:

  • Channel samples from North Zone returning up to 3.8% TREO over 8.5 meters
  • Surface grab samples from Central Zone yielding 4.2% TREO with high HREE content
  • South Zone revealing dysprosium values up to 985 ppm and terbium up to 175 ppm
  • Preliminary metallurgical testing indicating potential recoveries above 75%

These results support the project's potential to host a significant rare earth element resource with favorable distribution of critical REEs required for permanent magnets and other high-tech applications.

Environment & Community

Golden Goose Exploration is committed to responsible resource development in the James Bay region, with a strong focus on environmental protection and meaningful engagement with Indigenous communities. Our approach includes:

  • Early and ongoing engagement with local First Nations communities
  • Incorporation of traditional knowledge into environmental studies
  • Commitment to training and employment opportunities for local residents
  • Minimization of project footprint and implementation of progressive reclamation
  • Development of a water management plan that protects local watersheds

The company has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the local Cree Nation, establishing a framework for collaboration throughout the project lifecycle. This agreement includes provisions for environmental protection, cultural heritage preservation, training, employment, and business opportunities.

In Quebec's mining-friendly jurisdiction, we are working closely with provincial authorities to ensure compliance with all regulatory requirements while advancing the project in a sustainable manner.

Strategic Importance

Rare earth elements are classified as critical minerals by the Canadian, U.S., and European governments due to their essential role in clean energy technologies and national security applications. The Quebec Claim project is strategically important for several reasons:

  • Contains significant concentrations of "magnet rare earths" (Nd, Pr, Dy, Tb) essential for EV motors and wind turbines
  • Located in a stable, mining-friendly jurisdiction with excellent infrastructure
  • Potential to support North American rare earth supply chain development
  • Diversification of global REE supplies currently dominated by China

The project benefits from Quebec's clean hydroelectric power, which offers the potential for low-carbon REE production compared to global competitors, aligning with the growing demand for responsibly-sourced critical minerals.
