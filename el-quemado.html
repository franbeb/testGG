El Quemado Lithium Project

Salta Province, Argentina

Our flagship lithium project located in the heart of South America's "Lithium Triangle", targeting high-grade lithium-bearing pegmatite formations with excellent development potential.

Project Overview

The El Quemado Lithium Project encompasses 4,200 hectares of highly prospective land in the Salta Province of northwestern Argentina. The project is strategically located within South America's renowned "Lithium Triangle", which hosts approximately 60% of the world's lithium resources.

Initial surveys and exploration work have identified multiple lithium-bearing pegmatite formations with significant concentrations of spodumene, the primary lithium-bearing mineral. Golden Goose Exploration acquired the El Quemado property in 2020 and has since conducted extensive geological mapping, geophysical surveys, and preliminary drilling to delineate the resource potential. The company is currently advancing the project through a comprehensive exploration and development program aimed at establishing a maiden resource estimate by late 2024.

Geology & Mineralogy

The El Quemado property is characterized by several large pegmatite bodies intruded into metamorphic host rocks. The pegmatites are predominantly composed of quartz, feldspar, and mica, with significant concentrations of spodumene, the primary lithium-bearing mineral of economic interest.

Geological mapping and sampling have identified at least seven major pegmatite dykes across the property, ranging from 5 to 25 meters in width and extending for several hundred meters along strike. The pegmatites are exposed at surface in numerous locations, facilitating cost-effective exploration and future mining operations.

Mineralogical studies have confirmed that spodumene is the dominant lithium-bearing mineral, with crystals typically ranging from 2 to 15 cm in length. Additional valuable minerals identified include tantalite, beryl, and cassiterite, which may provide valuable by-product credits in future mining operations.

Exploration & Development

Golden Goose Exploration has implemented a systematic exploration program at El Quemado, including:

  • Detailed geological mapping and surface sampling across the entire property
  • High-resolution geophysical surveys including magnetic and radiometric surveys
  • Trenching and channel sampling of exposed pegmatite bodies
  • Initial diamond drilling program consisting of 25 holes totaling 3,500 meters
  • Preliminary metallurgical testing to assess lithium recoverability
  • Environmental baseline studies in preparation for future development activities
