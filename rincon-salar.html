Rincon Salar Lithium Brine Project

Salta Province, Argentina

A high-potential lithium brine project in the renowned Rincon Salar basin of Argentina's "Lithium Triangle", targeting sustainable lithium extraction using advanced Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) technology.

Project Overview

The Rincon Salar Lithium Brine Project covers 3,500 hectares in the heart of the Rincon salt flat (salar) in northwestern Argentina. Located in the world-famous "Lithium Triangle," this project targets lithium-rich brines contained within the salt flat basin.

Initial sampling and testing have revealed lithium concentrations ranging from 350 to 400 mg/L, placing Rincon among the more prospective lithium brine projects in the region. The project benefits from excellent infrastructure, with paved highway access, proximity to rail lines, and available power supply.

Golden Goose Exploration acquired the Rincon Salar property in 2021 and has conducted extensive hydrogeological studies, brine sampling programs, and preliminary pump tests to evaluate the resource potential. The company is advancing the project with a focus on using environmentally-friendly Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) technology to minimize water consumption and environmental footprint.

Geology & Hydrogeology

Rincon Salar is a closed basin formed during the uplift of the Andes Mountains. The salar contains a multi-layered aquifer system consisting of salt layers interbedded with clay and sand units that host lithium-rich brines.

Hydrogeological studies have identified a substantial brine aquifer system extending to depths of over 300 meters. The brine chemistry is favorable, with relatively low magnesium-to-lithium ratios (Mg:Li approximately 6:1), making the resource amenable to conventional processing methods as well as newer DLE technologies.

The basin is fed by an extensive drainage system that brings lithium and other elements leached from the surrounding volcanic rocks into the salar. This continuous process has created substantial lithium concentrations over geological time.

Exploration & Development

Golden Goose Exploration has implemented a systematic exploration program at Rincon Salar, including:

  • Detailed geological and hydrogeological mapping of the salar basin
  • Geophysical surveys including TEM (Transient Electromagnetic) to define aquifer architecture
  • Systematic shallow and deep brine sampling program
  • Installation of monitoring wells and initial pump testing
  • Preliminary laboratory testing of Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) technologies
  • Environmental baseline studies and water balance assessment

Initial exploration results have been encouraging, with highlight findings including:

  • Average lithium grades of 375 mg/L across multiple sampling points
  • Favorable brine chemistry with manageable impurity levels
  • Consistent aquifer properties indicating good potential for brine extraction
  • Identification of a potential 250-meter thick productive aquifer zone

These results support the project's potential to host a significant lithium brine resource with favorable development characteristics.

Environment & Sustainability

Golden Goose Exploration is committed to developing the Rincon Salar Project with industry-leading environmental standards. Traditional lithium brine operations rely on evaporation ponds that consume large amounts of water in an arid region. Our development approach focuses on Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) technology that offers numerous advantages:

  • Significantly reduced water consumption compared to traditional evaporation methods
  • Smaller physical footprint with minimal surface disturbance
  • Potential for brine reinjection to maintain aquifer balance
  • Accelerated production timeline with higher lithium recovery rates
  • Lower carbon footprint and energy requirements

The company is working with local communities and regional authorities to ensure sustainable development that benefits all stakeholders. Our commitment includes comprehensive water monitoring, transparent environmental reporting, and active community engagement throughout the project lifecycle.
